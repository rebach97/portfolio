About Me

I am an Alabama-based journalist with five years of experience in statewide and national newsrooms, specializing in investigative reporting, public records, and data-driven storytelling. My reporting focuses on education equity, policy, and systemic challenges impacting schools and communities across the South.

I currently serve as AL.com's education and data reporter, leading the education team's K-12 coverage and assisting journalists across the newsroom with data analysis and visualization. I joined AL.com in 2021 as a Report for America corps member, where I was a founding member of the outlet's Education Lab, focusing on opportunity gaps and equity issues in Alabama schools. My prior journalism experience includes a yearlong stint on the New York Times' Pulitzer-winning COVID-19 Tracking Project, where I covered the pandemic's impact on incarcerated populations. I am a two-time graduate of the University of Alabama, where I spent nearly five years at the student newspaper as a designer, reporter and editor, leading the newspaper's digital transition as the 2020-2021 editor-in-chief.

Selected Clips

One Alabama School System Responds to Rise in Immigration

Data-driven package examining the rise of immigrant populations in Alabama schools, presented at EWA and NAHJ seminars. To report this package, I used R/RStudio and tidycensus to combine nearly 30 years of state and federal enrollment data as well as immigration statistics from the American Community Survey.

CRT Debate Repeats Past Battles About State History Textbooks

Investigative feature tracing a century of Alabama textbook controversies and how they shape today’s classroom debates.

Alabama Mom Alleges School Retaliation

Exclusive investigation uncovering patterns of parental retaliation, leading to a federal class-action lawsuit against the U.S. Department of Education.

Brown v. Board: What Was Left Behind

Historical series reflecting on desegregation’s legacy and efforts to preserve Alabama’s Rosenwald schools.

Paraprofessionals are Key to a Reading Intervention Plan in Alabama

This story on a rural Alabama district’s reading gains was part of national Ed Lab Collaboration that was a finalist for a national headliner award. I spoke on EWA radio about this story with Jill Barshay from The Hechinger Report. A state board member called the story a “roadmap” for other Alabama schools to follow.

Two Alabama Community Colleges Take a New Approach to Advising

My coverage of efforts to raise community college completion rates was part of a national Ed Lab Collaboration. It was cited by a leading college access organization to advocate for equitable funding of HBCCs, and it was praised by The Grade for its data-focused look at a local solution.

Tackling Teacher Shortages: One Alabama Town’s Creative Approach

Solutions package with Ed Lab Collab was a finalist for EWA collaborations, and a judge specifically pointed out the Alabama story. Was called “very powerful” by the superintendent.

The ‘Best Gift’: Alabama School Celebrates Final Graduation

This story, co-reported with Trisha Powell Crain, follows one small school’s final graduation and makes the case that Black schools continue to bear the brunt of present-day desegregation efforts.

Alabama Cuts More Pre-K Equity Resources

This quick-turn accountability story relied on an analysis of state reading materials and training documentation to uncover the fallout of the controversial ousting of a Black state pre-K leader.

Alabama’s Anti-DEI Law Kicks In: College Offices Close, Websites Scrubbed

This was the first, and most comprehensive, look at the impact of Alabama’s anti-DEI law on college campuses in the state. Two students featured in this story were contacted by the NAACP to serve as plaintiffs in a court case against the State of Alabama and The University of Alabama.
