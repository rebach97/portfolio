About Me

I am an Alabama-based journalist with five years of experience in statewide and national newsrooms, where I have specialized in data-driven storytelling. My reporting has focused on education equity, criminal justice and other systemic challenges and solutions impacting communities across the South.

I currently serve as AL.com's education and data reporter, leading the education team's K-12 coverage and assisting journalists across the newsroom with data analysis and visualization. I joined AL.com in 2021 after a yearlong stint with the New York Times' Pulitzer-winning COVID-19 Tracking Project, where I covered the pandemic's impact on incarcerated populations.

My journalism career started in high school, where I covered everything from drunk driving to bad school plays to school segregation for my school paper, the Northridge Reporter. I have also covered Memphis schools as a Chalkbeat intern, worked on elections databases, and led a college newspaper through a pandemic, campus protests, administrative turnover, a digital transition and Machine shennanigans. I live in Birmingham with my boyfriend, Bret, and our spoiled pit mix, Albert.
